Final project: Update 1 — A basic idea

My idea for my final project is to turn (literally, on the lathe) a piece of wood to make a mechanical pencil body. The mechanism will be taken from a Pentel Sharp P20x (P207 specifically, denoting 0.7 mm lead).

It’s actually like the perfect pencil for this, as it doesn’t rely on some obscure geometry for the mechanism to work; it only needs a thinner section/lip to catch on near the tip so that the thing doesn’t slide out of the body. Furthermore, the tip screws into the mechanism itself, not the body, so that wouldn’t need to be re-made.

Despite it costing only about $5, it’s really well made. For example, the Spoke pencils and these IJ Instruments ones use the same mechanism. Oh, and the missing chunk of mine is just from me wanting to see where the insides were “mounted” on the body.

Ideally, the design would mimic that of a regular wooden pencil—hexagonal shape, but a wood texture. Perhaps slightly thicker, and maybe something different for the grip. I thought about using metal, but that would require using the 3D CNC machine, and that seems like a lot of work. Unless we figure out how it works, wood will do.

Mini project 1: Laser cutter — Keychain

This first mini project was to cut a keychain or small design on some wood with the laser cutter. Mine is a little exit sign based off of the signage found in the New York City subway, specifically from the NYCTA Graphics Standards Manual, the 1970s manual about the subway’s signage which is still used today.

I actually own a reprint of the book, and it’s very interesting if you’re into that sort of thing. Back to the point, I want to bring up that the laser cutter made an almost checkerboard pattern for the gray area. I suspect if I choose 500 DPI (instead of 300), the text would be crisper. Currently, it looks almost pixelated because of what the laser has to do to make it a different shade. It was made with just some boxes and text in Inkscape—I don’t really think there’s much else to report there, besides that the laser cutter doesn’t seem to handle clipped areas too well; they’d just disappear from the print preview.
